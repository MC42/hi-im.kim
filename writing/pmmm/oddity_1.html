The time traveler was asleep, or rather, was trying to sleep. Visions of nightmares swam in her unconscious mind, yet another loop where everything had gone ass-over-teakettle rather quickly. At the edge of her conscious mind, she felt a poke, little more than a telepathic pins-and-needles, a declaration of intent of sorts, and yet it was enough.

Homura felt the familiar and discomforting pressure of an Incubator on her torso, transformed and weapon drawn in an instant. The interloper removed itself with urgency, seemingly aware of the immediate danger it was in. Where the scenario had diverged was, of course, that the telepathic carrier was carrying a different voice.

Sorry transfer student, this is important. End of the world important, I know you love that sorta thing.

What the hell?

Homura was staring, rather unceremoniously, at an Incubator with blue eyes.

She had hoped she could have dismissed it as an abberant Incubator, a statistical anomaly. Perhaps a body for their collective had splintered, cursed with the human affliction of individualized emotions. She almost cherished the thought for a moment, but found herself doubting the possibility.

After all, the strange Incubator had a soul gem on her neck.

A brilliant blue stone set not in a gold or silver filgree, but a rough gem wrapped in something almost organic. It was attached to the white-furred frame by an aquamarine ribbon tied in a delicate bow.

If the assertion of identity in form of a gemstone hadn’t been enough on the cabbity frame, the absence of those traditional golden rings sealed the sheer absurdity of what she was looking at.

“Wh-what? What happened to you Sayaka-chan?”

Sayaka tilted her head, using a manipulator to scratch her head in a way that unmistakably was imprecise.

“Stuff broke, I guess. This little bubble universe cut a few million people and a few thousand magical girls off from her wish, and the last thing we need is Incubators figuring out the secret.”

Her mouth wasn’t moving, emotive thoughts carring over telepathy with the same cadence and tone as she had when they so unceremoniously had their spats in that distant past.

“No, I mean… why do you look like that.”

“I had to… uh… improvise? Try not to shoot me on sight please?”

Homura stared down and realized she was, against her better judgement, still cradling a beretta.

“Sorry.” she mumbled absentmindedly, lowering the beretta such that if she did fire it, it’d only hit her paws.

“No hard feelings, we fought a lot, right?” the Incubator smiled, a disturbing warmth carried on a frame typically afflicted by a mortis smile devoid of emotion.

Sayaka almost seemed remorseful in the moment, but Homura was despite her best efforts, confused. After all, she was addressing “the fate of the universe” with the same intonation as a late school assignment.

“R-Right. So… how do we uh… save the universe?”

“See that’s the problem. I can’t say exactly. I can push my perspective a little bit beyond causal responsibility but… there are consequences to messing with time.”

“Causal responsibility? The Sayaka Miki I knew never knew the meaning of the phrase.”

“The Sayaka Miki you knew had a life expectancy measured in weeks.”

Homura frowned slightly, creases returning to a face both younger than it looked and more worn than it should be.

“So you’re borrowing an Incubator’s body, I’m missing major parts of my memory, and we’re nearly four hundred years and several thousand light-years from home trying to prevent the knowledge of… you know, from spreading.”

“That sums it up, I think, yeah.”

If she didn’t know better, if she didn’t know Sayaka Miki better, she almost sounded smug. She shook her head, realizing just how hard it was going to be to separate her past from this… development. Nevertheless, she had to try, for Madoka.

Homura holstered her beloved beretta in her buckler and banished the set with ease. The esteemed magical girl was left wearing, without further explanation, a borrowed set of pajamas. She paused for a moment as she stared down at the pattern on the tee, finding the design both familiar and melancholy. Deep in thought, she tried and tried to find an origin, to no avail.

Is that… a grief seed? What jerk would put a grief seed pattern as the logo for a magical girl organization!?

Her attention was only seized when she realized, rather abruptly, that the sweet aroma of pancakes was wafting in from the kitchen.

“Oh, and word of advice? Don’t ask for butter on your pancakes.”

“What does—”

“It breaks the timeline. Gotta go, be back later.” she said, vanishing with the same impunity the Mitakihara Incubator was known for, leaving the transfer student alone with her thoughts once more.

“Jerk…”
