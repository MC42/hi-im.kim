Chapter 1 – Rhyme

 "It is nice to know that the universe is self‐consistent enough to handle a few small exploits, a few violations of its laws here and there, without falling apart entirely. After all, when you have all of reality to run, what's a few ontological paradoxes among friends?"

“The complete disappearance of the Galileo-µ colony is a tragedy of enormous significance.  Despite the resounding effects of the scenario encountered in Kepler-37, our best and brightest cannot conclusively establish a root cause for the colony’s disappearing act.  While quite a few suspect it was wormhole-related, and some would attribute it to the instability of the entire sector, some better informed as of late suspect it had something to do with a wish.”

 
August was no stranger to the sound of gunfire, after all, it’d been her alarm clock for what felt like years.  Staring blurry-eyed at the horizon, she found exactly what she expected, quietly resigned at the unarguable reality.  Staring back at her was an inky-black sky, not a single star in sight.  Through gritted teeth, she shook her sister, June, awake, the girl bleary-eyed and squinting through the dust-filled air.

“Is it morning…?” June mumbled, her curly mop of hair threatening to obscure her vision.

“Something like that.  C’mon.” August smiled back, her glasses hiding the bags under her eyes as well as any could.

The two of them snuck through the debris field as carefully as they could, their gray coveralls the same color as the rubble.  Methodical steps, one after the other, took them through the collapsed surroundings.  They couldn’t afford the bravado that others their age might’ve, and were significantly more fragile, even with the myriad of implants and upgrades that percolated to the outer colonies.

August grasped at the edge of a streetlight, knowing based on the shape of the dome that they found a subway entrance.  Despite the lack of other obvious signage, she figured it was as clear a sign as they’d get.  If they could sneak through the entrance, they’d be able to get somewhere safe, to find loved ones.  August hoped desperately that Galileo survived.  All they had to do was wait for the train.  

As August climbed past concrete boulders, alloy reinforcements still jutting through the wreckage like barbs, she didn’t once let go of June’s hand.  They were safer together, after all, despite the occasional mis-step.

The stairs to the station were utterly charred, detonated explosives spalling the concrete, the damage only waning once they descended the final stairwell onto the platform itself.  It seemed that, despite the efforts of the Ceph, this station had survived intact, and in fairly good shape, dust notwithstanding.

The tunnels that cross-connected the planet were carved from the rock-face, stitches beneath the surface, struggling to keep the world together.  The speed of the trains traversing them left a hollow wind sweeping through the station, a constant breeze keeping them company while they waited.  While a station normally had platform gates, any that might’ve been present were removed in the days and months after the world vanished.

August had ridden the train with her parents enough to know about the wind, June too young to remember it as anything more than a dream.  A dream from when stars still shone in the sky.

June couldn’t help but shudder in the wind, colder for it.

“I don’t like this…”  June murmured, her sister squeezing her hand tighter.

“Me neither Juney, but you gotta be strong, okay?”

“Easy for you to say!  You’re older!”

August couldn’t help but laugh a little.  “I may be older but that doesn’t make it easier.” neglecting to continue the thought.  Besides.  At least you don’t have to remember Mom and Dad so vividly.

As she stood on the platform, hoping that June couldn’t see her face through her own hair tossed in the wind, she heard the gentle rumble of the train in the distance.  The tunnel walls echoed, making every interaction of the track and wheel resonate as if a drum, as August heard the carriage begin deceleration in the distance.  The tracks sung in harmony with the speed of the car as the brakes “plucked” their guides; a steel alloy guitar string hundreds of kilometers long.

Through distorted speakers, the automated announcement system spat out in Standard a crackling, crunching message, distorted through what must’ve been damaged speakers.  “This is an Express, Pink Line Train to New Florence.  First stop will be, Chapel Hill.”

The girls got on, their worn sneakers crossing from the weathered concrete into the comparatively pristine carriage, dust settling in their footsteps.  It wasn’t hard to find a seat, the train deserted spare them, every seat an option.  As the doors sealed with a pleasant chime, the lighting inside the carriage flickering slightly as the motors hummed to life.  With a lurch, and then the discordant tone of the motors accelerating, their train careened through the dark towards New Florence.

As the carriage continued its feverish pace into the subterranean void, rocking gently on the tracks, June couldn’t help but nod off.  It was the most comfortable place they’d been in weeks, and it had HVAC.  August reached into her knapsack and pulled out a water bottle, the seal of her parent’s workplace still emblazoned on the bottom, corroding in the uncontrolled ecosystem.  

Still, as far as August was concerned, this was luxury.

I hope that things are better in New Florence.  Maybe they’ll use words and not gunfire.

As she stared through the discolored front window of the carriage, she found herself staring at a bullethole she hadn’t noticed in the station, aimed up towards the ceiling.

Or at least, not exclusively gunfire, August silently prayed to no one in particular.

 
“So we’ve got Halley and Brahm moved to Nova Sicilia, are we forgetting anyone?”

“We could go back for Galileo, they are the colony’s namesake.   Could encourage their peers to cooperate.”

“Galileo retired, boss.  Like, a day or two before we got sent into this hellhole.  We don’t need to get ‘em.  As far as their peers are concerned, they’re probably dead anyways.”

The girl paused for a moment, fidgeting with a ring on her finger, hoping that her subordinates wouldn’t notice.  If they did, she’d deal with it with the only tools she had left; force.

“Fine.  But you better check in a week or two if the datacenter is fully destroyed.  Last thing we need is someone to spread rumors, especially some spread by Governance sentients.”
 
A pleasant chime woke August up, and she found the point of a spear hovering centimeters from her nose.  A spear held still by a girl in a frilly costume, looking slightly older than August.  

A magical girl.  Of course.  This is MSY territory, or… Church territory?  Is there a difference?

“Who are you?” the girl balked, her hair kept neatly in twin ponytails, a pale orange skirt matching the ends of her hair, her eyes a deeper amber, and of course, her soul gem shone in the same shade.  The girl was trying her best to be threatening, but, August was still slightly taller, despite the mage’s heels.

“Uh.  I’m August-”

“Wrong question I guess.  Why are you here.” she demanded, August wearily standing her ground as June shrunk behind her slightly.  

“Safety hopefully. We grew up on the outskirts of Bergen, which-”

“Was destroyed in the Ceph attack...” the magical girl finished, her spear dissolving as easily as she’d summoned it.  Her posture faltered for a moment, staring past the girls into the interior of the carriage, August realizing suddenly there were two more magical girls on the outside of the carriage.  A glance to the front of the train found her staring at a nervous rail system worker, the older man holding an override button at the staff gate towards the end of the station.

“We’ve been trying to get to somewhere safe, and a few folks we found on the outskirts points us in the right direction, most of the other trains broke down though, so we had to go on foot until we… got here I guess.”

Through a silent and exasperated sigh, the magical girl around and walked out of the car, waving the two girls standing guard to follow her.  The mechanical operator released the hold button, the traincar slowly rolling to the next stop.

Isn’t waving those magical girls to follow on a little superfluous?  They can telepathically talk, right..?

As they ascended the stairs, their once-aggressor pulled out a keyring from an unseen pocket of her skirt, methodically opening a door labeled “Staff Only | D&E” with the twist of a key, descending into what August hoped was just a maintenance pathway.

As August turned the corner of the catwalk, June turned around to look at the open door, the two guards looking at her with the faintest hints of pity as the door closed, the lock clicking shut behind them.

They snaked their way through back tunnels, walkways barely tall enough for them, girders and spans decaying slightly in the environment less controlled as some of it revealed sunlight, cascading down the practically incandescent cavern walls, the red bouncing back up off the floor, casting distorted shadows on the far wall.

“So uh-” August started, the girl in front of her not acknowledging her talking.

“Where are we going?” she asked, the wall as responsive as their escort, before finally responding.

“Somewhere safe.” the magical girl said in a defeated tone.

“I thought all of New Florence was safe?” August cautiously raised, the girl’s posture deflating slightly.

“It is!” she snipped, continuing before her companions had a chance to respond.   “Or rather… it was like that.  For a while anyways.  We can’t just take in every single sob-story that comes our way.  Some want to hurt others.”

Even June couldn’t ignore the undertones, the barely-veiled threat.  

Others who we took mercy on betrayed us.  Don’t make the same mistake.

The silence hung heavy in the air, punctuated largely by the pipework, the crunch of gravel under foot, and winces as they ducked and weaved through the maze of tunnels.

June was tired of the rattle of pipes and the arrhythmic thud of footsteps being the only sounds in the air, and decided to test their guide.

“Why is this place so old?  It feels like something ancient, something cursed.”

The magical girl turned to face them as she responded, almost taking pride in the knowledge.

“You’re a smart kid, ya know?  It’s older than you’d think.”

June couldn’t help but smile before the orange-hued girl continued.

 “New Florence is like old Florence in that way.  Layers on layers, y’know?”

“Uh… no.” June mumbled, her smile mired by confusion, hoping the pretty lady wouldn’t mind.

August realized with an all-too-embarrassing epiphany that the magical girl was distrustful of them because she just had nothing to go on.  They were all too on edge.

After what felt like an eternity, and several dozen more turns, their escort was the one to open a conversation, a proverbial white flag in their cold war of sulking.

“It’s only fair you know where we’re going, I did hold you at spear-point, after all.” the magical girl admitted, hoping they’d take the hint.

“Hmpf.” June responded, August trying to shrug it off on her behalf.  

I earned that one I guess.  Nobody here takes kindly to being woken up at weapon-range.  Still, had to be careful.

“I’m taking you through the tunnels to MSY District.  You said you were running, do you have family here?”

“Uh… kinda?  We were adopted in Nova Sicilia by the Kisakis but…” August trailed off, catching the gaze of the magical girl, her eyes dimly glowing in the dark, amber gems in the unrelenting void.

She didn’t need to finish the sentence, the guilt weighed on her enough that she visibly deflated as the sentence trailed off.

“Okay.” their guide mused, expecting something of the sort.

The trio exited a door through another locked doorway, finding themselves in a sterile, but human friendly hallway.  A far cry from the proto-catacombs they’d been in just prior, August couldn’t help but breathe a sigh of relief.

“For the time being then, you’re staying with me.” she smiled, the door at the end of the hallway unlocking and opening itself.  As August and June passed the doorframe, they couldn’t help but read the names on the door. In both Human Standard and Japanese kanji the sign scribbled “Karina & Shannon Luhr”, June trying to hide a smile at the handwriting, her face washed over with awe as she entered the home.

They saw a kitchen with a full island, couches in a sprawling living room, one overflowing with all sorts of plush animals.  Karina briefly mentioned the multiple private bedrooms, guest rooms, tall ceilings, a dedicated study.  The Luhr household was luxurious by most standards, but August was most surprised by the food.  She had spices, and flours, all sorts of luxury goods.  Stuff from before the collapse, stuff that was practically unheard of on this rock.  Saffron, jasmine, liquors and more, a collection that’d cause wars a few hundred years prior, just sitting in neatly organized containers, begging to be used.

It felt like a home, not just a house.

“Excuse me, miss magical girl?”

“Mhmm, June, isn’t it?”

The girl nodded excitedly, the magical girl a celebrity to her.

“June, please call me Karina.” the girl’s inexplicably placid as she shed a black and silver overcoat, utterly useless for a magical girl like her, tucking it into the nearby closet as she walked past.  Moving past the entryway, she strayed into her living room, sitting down in an armchair methodically.

“Miss Karina, can my sister make you some food?  She’s a really good cook!” June smiled, the charm turned to eleven for their savior of the hour.  Karina’s stomach couldn’t help but rumble.

“Hey!” August balked, “you could have asked first…”

June could help but shrug, “You’d never say no to that… didn’t think I needed to!”

“Oh, that’s not-” Karina balked.  There’s no way to tell them I practically eat garbage most of the time.  That this is my wife’s house more than my own.  That she might not-
“Are you sure, Luhr-sama?  It’d be no trouble at all, especially since you’re going through the trouble of hosting us.  Please.” August beckoned, the tension rising until their host’s polite posturing collapsed.  She was spent.

“Fine!  Fine.  You can cook whatever you want for yourselves, and if there’s leftovers I’ll happily take them.”  She couldn’t help but smile, the girls practically ecstatic at the opportunity.

“I still get food from the Church or work most of the time, rations or prepped meals are far more efficient. Don’t worry if you’re too hungry to leave something for me.  I promise it’s okay.” she beamed, hoping they got the message she was trying to lay out.  

August couldn’t help but notice the emphasis on the word “Church”, distinct over the generic of a chapel; August refused to make a face as Karina was still in deep thought over the expected kitchen antics, trying to remember where the cleaning supplies were.

No matter how bad it is, I’ll eat it.  Goddess knows I’ve made worse for Shannon, and eaten worse in combat.

“Thank you, Ms. Karina!” June giddily balked, as Mrs. Luhr found herself nursing a headache.

“I’m going to lay down for a bit." she mumbled, her face obscuring her head as she gingerly got to her feet, using the wall as a guide as she slowly ascended the steps to the half-landing.  “There’s clothes in the bedroom next to mine, something should fit both of you, toss your laundry in the hamper next to the bathroom.”

“Thank you again, Luhr-sama!” August and June said in unison, looking at the kitchen’s pantry with glee, August pulling out an actual paper and ink cookbook as Karina collapsed into bed.

Goddess-damnit.  Why do I keep taking in strays?

She pulled open a drawer and pulled out grief cubes, her soul gem murky.  Such was the cost of navigating the maze of tunnels under the city.  She’d paid that penalty more than once, but this much depletion was sloppy, even by her admittedly waning standards.

Know the enemy, keep it together.

 
Karina woke to the smell of bread.  Actual bread, made the old fashioned way.  She had more than her fair share of allocs between her magical girl dispensation and Shannon’s in their shared account, but having the time to go and get fresh food when visiting was never really an option.  Shannon loved to surprise her, jams and breads, rolls and sandwiches.  Actual non-synthesized, handmade food.
As she swung her bedroom door open, she found August and June happily plodding along in the kitchen.  Two rolls of something brilliantly aromatic sat on the counter, split open already with June happily devouring a sandwich, August enjoying some toast of some kind, preserves spread across the surface.

“How long was I out?” she asked, her internal chronometer happily spitting out an answer of 17 hours, three minutes.

“It’s about mid-day so, probably like fifteen, sixteen hours?” August said, her affect a little more cheery than it had been the day before. Finally able to relax a little.

“Ouch, guess I was more stressed than I thought.”

“I mean, who wouldn’t be, finding two vagrant children on a train that might’ve been a bomb or something.” August joked, a smile creeping in.

“Ouch, rude!” Karina smiled back, taking it all in jest.  She had to.

After a moment, and grabbing a slice of the bread, she nibbled on it absentmindedly for a while.  

It’s good.  The herbs and butter, crunch and give.  It’s delicious.

She was so thrown off she almost didn’t notice the girls watching her enjoy the fruits of their labor, hoping so much it’d be taken well.

“You two sleep okay?” Karina mumbled, bread still in her mouth as she relished the novelty.   Still easier to get than the synthesized equivalent.

“I slept like a rock…” August chirped, June groaning slightly.

“I had… weird dreams.  Bleh.” June groused, knowing full well how childish it made her look.  She didn’t care.

“How so?” Karina asked, knowing full well she had nightmares too, and hoping she hadn’t given any to the kids.

“Uh… there was a cat, I think?  Gunfire.  A bunch of people screaming.  And then something so big it made the sun go dark.”

August’s grip faltered, her sandwich slipping, if for a moment, catching it right before it hit the counter.  A dream like that sounded more like…

August’s reaction caught Karina off-guard, but she was determined to not show it.

It was almost as if she knows how this works.  Better than she should.

“I-…  I know we got off on the wrong foot yesterday.” Luhr-sama huffed.

“What an understatement…” June mumbled, Karina raising her hand slightly to protest, before falling back down.

“I… earned that one.  So, my name is Karina.  This is my home, and my wife’s of course.  I wor-”

“Where is she?” August asked, Karina’s face tensing at the interruption.

Another uneasy silence later, she continued, careful to not make herself upset.  Last thing she wanted was emotional suppression around kids.

“She… died.  We think.   They’re not sure.” she muttered, her voice so quiet they could barely hear.

“I’m so sorry. I-we, didn’t know.” August sputtered, June nodding along intentionally.

“Don’t be.  We both signed up for this, y’know?  She was on her way back from Kepler-37, trying to outrun it all and, she wasn’t inside before the teardrop formed.”

August couldn’t help but grimace, she’d kept digging and got exactly what she asked for.

June raised her hand as if to answer a question in class, Karina nodding at her, expectantly.

“Luhr-sama, uh… how old are you?”

“June!  You can’t just ask people that.”

“No no, it’s okay.” Karina laughed.  

This kid is bold and nosy.  I like that.

“I’m actually twenty-seven.”

“Wait, but you look our age.” August said, jaw half-agape.

“Hah, I sure don’t get treated it most of the time!” Karina laughed, a weak smile floating back to the surface.  June and August were disarming in a way she found uncanny.  She checked for magic in the area, and found nothing unexpected.  

So they’re not enchanted or something.  Phew. I just got Shannon’s love of kids, which… ugh.

“It’s pretty common for magical girls to be older than they look, especially for the really old ones.  They can be older than most grandparents and still look like you or me.”

“Whoa…” June said, starry-eyed as August shaking her head briefly as she tried to focus on the tasks occupying her mind.

“So, what’s the plan for today then?” August asked, Karina finishing her piece of bread before grabbing another.

Karina contemplated just sending them an itinerary for her day, before realizing for civilians it’d be nearly entirely redacted, even if it was communicable.  Not as useful as just telling them verbally, even though she strictly didn’t need to.  Besides, they’re kids.

“So, I gotta drop you off at the Rehousing Group, talk to Stephanie.  She’s great, and will help you two three hots and a cot, so to speak.  Plus, you’ll find out how to get involved locally.”

“Okay, cool!”

“Uh, Luhr-sama?”

“Yes August?”

“Would it be okay if I went to visit a friend first?”

“Of course.  I already set an appointment for you for about four hours from now, so that should give them plenty of time.”

“You’re… just gonna drop us off and then ditch?”

She nodded, a weak smile on her face.  “Someone’s gotta do the work still.  We’re not gonna be in here forever.”

August took her bag from the floor near the door and slung it over her shoulder, the well-worn vessel pressing dirt and concrete dust into the clean maroon sweater she’d borrowed from Karina’s laundry pile.  She glanced back at the interior of the living space with a hopeful look on her face, before closing the door.

As they left the home, nestled between a stuffed shark and a throw pillow with a cheesy joke on it, a glassy-eyed off-white plush on the couch blinked at the door they’d just left through, and vanished.

 
The trio found themselves staring at the door that signaled their departure from the sprawling and maze-like underground, another “D&E | Staff Only” sign staring at them while Karina procured her keyring again.

“Luhr-sama, can I ask a question?” August piped up, Karina already a dozen steps down the catwalk.

“You already did though, what is it?” the magical girl smiled back, her eyes glowing a dim orange in the relative shade of the tunnel.  Still, the girls unable to ignore how much comfortable she was this trip, despite the maze of the catacombs.

“Uh… how is the district doing?  Or like… MSY as a whole here.  Don’t get in trouble or anything but, I want to know how safe my sister is… and you I think.” August rambled, biting her tongue on the last few syllables.

“Yeah, magical girls are the thing that keeps all of us safe, so are you safe?” June prattled off, nearly word-for-word a poster Karina had seen on the inside of some bulkhead a tour or two ago.

As June nearly bumped into her, Karina found herself a bit ashamed, reveling in the joy.  If for a moment, she got to forget there was at least one war on.

“So… we’re not doing great.  We met you at the station with an armed patrol, remember?”

Karina couldn’t help but reflect.  It felt absurd to do that, but the protocol dictated it, and there was precedent.

August and June nodded in unison as they recalled being woken up at spearpoint.  Rather quickly though, June found herself wearing a confused expression.

“I mean.  It did seem a bit odd though…” June warbled, “aren’t magical girls for big stuff, not just two random girls?”

“MSY has had folks show up like you did.  She vaporized a city block in Old City, set off a bomb of some kind.  Took us weeks to clean up the mess, a lot of people died...”
“Oh god, I’m so sorry, I didn’t-” August began, fighting her emotions as best she could.
“August.  Stop apologizing.  You had no way of knowing!  It was classified and stuff, and as civilians, you don’t get access that info.” Karina lamented, August on the verge of tears.

“What happened to not breaking rules?” June pressed, Karina taken aback by how observant the girl was.

“I… given the circumstances of, well… everything, we’re a bit short on staff.  We really need anyone we can get,  sorta like a TNC was back in the day.” Karina divulged, August’s melancholy interrupted by the absurdity of it all.

“TNC?” she asked, nose still sniffly.

“Trusted Non-Contractees.” Karina responded matter-of-factly.

“I thought you didn’t trust us?” June asked, a sour look on her face.

“I don’t.  I mean, not entirely.  Not yet.  You gotta do a background check, but they’ll do that at Rehousing.  We check everyone now, given the circumstances.” Karina added, each addition more rushed than the last.  She didn’t even notice she was distracted until they’d already taken a wrong turn, and was now very confused where they were.

“You didn’t murder me in my sleep, so you’re pretty good in my book.” she smiled, still mired with anxiety at the dizzying labyrinth in front of her.

Besides, you’re both contracting age.  If you’ve survived the past few months in this hellscape, you’re clever and intelligent.  We need clever just as much as we do magic right now.

The girls laughed a bit, August unable to stop herself from babbling on “You didn’t hurt us either, so how bad can you really be?”

As Karina stopped dead in her tracks, June and August bumped into her, the girl stumbling as she caught herself on the smooth rockface in front of her.

“Glad you trust me a little more, but I think I fucked up.” Karina admitted, the girls’ faces contorted in fear.

“I think we’re lost.” Karina mumbled, “I don’t know how but we’re lost.”

August reached into her bag for a flashlight as June picked a large flat rock off the ground, ready to fight.

“And I’ve got a feeling someone else is down here with us.” Karina couldn’t help but mumble, so quiet she hoped that her companions wouldn’t hear her.

 
“Wait, how did you get lost, you seemed like you’ve done this a bunch!”

“I have! But usually I’m alone, or with other magical girls.  Not just two chatty civilians!”

“Agh, I don’t like this!” June yelped

“No shit, Socrates!  Okay, give me a second and just be quiet.  August, turn your torch off.”

“Why?” August balked, their only source of light in the dark’s beam jittered slightly, following the shaking of her hand.

“Magical girl stuff, I swear.  Plus, it helps me focus.” Karina said, August nodding carefully.

“Okay…” August gulped, turning the flashlight off and plunging the tunnel into darkness, the environment only illuminated dimly by Karina’s eyes.

She squeezed her hand with June tighter, the girls nervous.

“Okay, we need to go back three intersections, and then up the stairs.  Follow me.” Karina stated absolutely, August flicking the flashlight back to its radiant operation, the warm glow distinct from the amber trickle they’d had moments prior.

“Why did you need it dark?”

“Uh.  Combat stuff lets me see in the dark.  Wider spectra, can even see in the infared.”

“Ah.” August nervously swallowed still, the minor shock still making her more on edge, her host already visibly relaxing.

“I don’t know how you just… recover so quickly.”

“Getting lost in tunnels isn’t the hardest thing most magical girls do in a normal week.  Besides, I was practically raised in tunnels like these.”

“Really?  How?  You must’ve been a kid!  And not like, the one you look now!” June sputtered, clearly trying to understand how the girl before them became a magical girl.  August rolled her eyes a little at the showmanship.

“Uh.  My parents worked for D&E.  I contracted as soon as an Incubator showed up.   They got the pamphlet and stuff explaining how it all worked, but they already knew.  Patriotic duty or something.”

“D&E?”

“Delivery and Errands.  Started as a Mitakihara delivery company, then all of Japan, and now they’re mostly a vestige of formality.  Some of the Ancients like to wax poetic and be nostalgic about the old days.”

“Oh.” August said.  “I didn’t know it was formed in Mitakihara.  I think one of the ancestors of our parents was from there.”

“August, the sooner you learn this the better.  A lot of stuff out here still has ties back to Mitakihara.” Karina huffed, her shoulders tensing as they found themselves at the door they’d been ushered through the day before, “lightly encouraged” at spearpoint.

“And here we are at the station.” Karina announced, distracted by something the girls couldn’t see as she pushed through the turnstile.  “Just stay on the train until it says something about housing or sanctuary.  Both stops get you to Rehousing.” Karina imparted as she turned towards the stairs. August and June watched as the girl transformed in an instant, acknowledging an alert on her TacComp.

“Next train is in two minutes.  See you later!   You should be able to get in to my place if anything goes wrong!” she yelled jumping back over the turnstile and sprinting up the stairs, so quickly that she left a cloud of dust as she left.

“Wait but I-” August began, Karina already long gone.

“What is it?” June asked, trying to console her sister.

“I wanted to say thank you… and to ask if Galileo was still working in the city center...”

“I thought Galileo was like… a super smart AI.” June piped, relaxing finally that it was her and her big sister again.

“Yeah! They are, but… they were always really nice when I needed help with my math homework.” August professed.

“But… I thought that was something Governance Ar-ith-ma-meti-”

“Arithmatic Education.  Yeah AriEd did help a bunch, but… Galileo didn’t make me feel dumb when I messed up...” she mused, reflecting on memories of her digital confidant.

 
The two girls found themselves in a small but very functional office nestled between a bombed out office complex and a small coffeeshop that somehow still had beans.  Not many, mind you, but still.

As they approached the front desk, the lady behind it gestured for them to wait a moment, as she hung up an audio call, hand gesture and all.  

“Hi uh… we’re looking for someone named Stephanie?  Uh… Luhr-sama told us you’d be able to get us a place to live.”

“Oh, you’re the two kids her patrol found on the train, huh.” she grumbled, her eyes slightly bloodshot as August had a chance to get a closer look.

“Yep that’s us!” August nervously laughed, hoping the woman would be able to help them.

“Well, I’m Stephanie.  Stephanie Diaz.  So, I’ll be honest, that puta was a bit misleading.  We don’t have all that many residences with open slots.  There’s three in total that are willing, and all of them are at least four person households.”

“Uh, does that include folks who are deployed or something?”

“Ordinarily, yes, but… allegedly MSY and the matriarch are in the middle of peace talks.  They expect her to renege on her vow of destruction.”

“Wait what?” June exclaimed.
